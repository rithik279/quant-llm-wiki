## Call Preparation (Before Aiden Call)

### Context

- First-year student at Rotman Commerce
- Proposed systematic research framework covering strategy research, regime detection, portfolio construction
- Had competing SF startup opportunity
- Target compensation: $35-40/hour
- Target hours: 20-30/week
- Must not sound unfocused or overcommitted

### Reading Aiden's Email

- "expand your ideas into a concrete plan" = testing execution ability
- "Work together" = collaboration signal, not interrogation
- Patrick's absence means Aiden is primary evaluator
- Core evaluation criteria: clarity of thought, coachability, ability to reduce Aiden's workload

### Call Structure

- Phase 1 (3-5 min): Opening — set serious, collaborative, low-ego tone
- Phase 2 (20-25 min): Substance — let Aiden share first, then anchor on concrete deliverables
- Phase 3 (5-10 min): Logistics — compensation, hours, timeline

### Key Phrases Scripted

- Opening: "I've been thinking more about the one-pager and have some ideas on how to scope it practically - happy to walk through those, but I'd also love to hear what you and Patrick have been discussing on your end first."
- Value signal: "What would be most useful to you in the first few weeks?"
- Comp anchor: "I'm targeting something in the $35-40 range, and I'd ideally be working 20-30 hours a week."

### SF Startup Handling

- Do NOT volunteer this information
- If asked: "I'm exploring one other opportunity - an early-stage startup - but nothing's finalized. I'm more interested in the research depth here."

## Post-Call Signals

- "We are very interested in you" = strong hiring intent
- "Willing to invest / flexible on comp" = budget not a constraint
- "Define what you need (data + compute)" = they want a builder
- "Take your time" = quality over speed
- "Happy to adjust around exams" = accommodating
- "If this works, we continue long-term" = path beyond internship
