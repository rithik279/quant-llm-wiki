# Alpha Math: Resource Justification Framework

## The $10M AUM Context

Fund AUM estimated at $10M+ for alpha math calculations.

## The Math

| Alpha Generated | Dollar Impact | Justifiable Spend |
|----------------|---------------|------------------|
| +0.5% | $50,000 | $5,000 |
| +1.0% | $100,000 | $10,000 |
| +5.0% | $500,000 | $50,000 |

## The Key Principle

> "If any resource has a 20% probability of adding 1% alpha, and the fund is $10M+, a $2,000 experiment has strongly positive expected value."

### Expected Value Calculation

- Probability: 20%
- Alpha added if successful: 1% ($100,000 on $10M)
- Cost: $2,000
- Expected value: (0.20 × $100,000) - $2,000 = $18,000
- **Expected ROI: 9x**

## Aiden's Stated Ranges

Per Aiden's email:
- "Anything under $5,000 is cheap... can be an experimental thing"
- "5% on a year round basis... we would be able to justify quite a bit of money like in the $50,000 range"
- "Don't let cost be your main factor here"

## What This Means

Any experiment under $5,000 is low-cost to Aiden. The fund can justify $50,000+ if you can prove 5% alpha.

Your job: Run experiments, measure alpha, prove value. Cost is not the constraint.

## Resource Justification Template

When proposing a resource:

```
Resource: [Name]
Cost: $[Amount]

```
