# Academic Strategy: Management Specialist vs Finance & Economics

## The Decision

**Choice:** Management Specialist with Finance Focus

**Why:** Same finance access, 2.5 FCE freed up for technical courses.

## Two Options Compared

1. **Finance & Economics Specialist**
2. **Management Specialist** (with Finance Focus)

## Year 1 Completed (5.0 FCE)

- MAT135 + MAT136 (Calculus)
- CSC108 (Intro to CS)
- STA130 (Intro Stats)
- RSM100, ECO101, ECO102

## The Key Difference

| Specialist | ECO Requirements | Net FCE Available |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Finance & Economics | 5.5 FCE | 0 extra |
| Management | 3.0 FCE | 2.5 FCE freed |

**Management frees 2.5 FCE** (5 courses) for technical courses.

## What Finance Access Looks Like

Finance access is IDENTICAL in both paths. Both include:
- RSM332 (Capital Markets)
- RSM333 (Corporate Finance)
- Access to upper-level finance electives

Finance & Econ adds RSM336 as requirement, but this can be taken under Management anyway.

## The Real Tax in Finance & Economics

The extra 2.5 FCE in Finance & Econ goes to:
- **ECO208Y/209Y** — Macroeconomic Theory (full-year course)
- **2.5 FCE of 300+ ECO electives** — 5 courses like Trade Theory, Development Macro

These are academically rigorous but not relevant to AI/ML engineering path.

## What 2.5 FCE Gets You

The freed courses can be used for:
- **CSC148** — Data Structures (critical for ML)
- **CSC207** — Software Design (critical for engineering)
- **MAT223** — Linear Algebra (critical for ML)
- **MAT235/237** — Multivariable Calculus
- **STA257/261** or **STA237/238** — Probability/Statistics sequence

## CR/NCR Strategy

2.0 FCE of Credit/No-Credit available. Strategy:

**Do NOT CR/NCR:**
- RSM courses (need grades)
- CSC148/207 (proves coding ability)

**CONSIDER CR/NCR:**
- MAT237 (hard)
- Upper-year CS courses (CSC311, CSC413)
- Advanced stats (STA302, STA414)

Allows aggressive technical course loading without GPA risk.

## Degree Marketing

| Context | What to Say |

| LinkedIn | "Rotman Commerce, Finance & Statistics" |
| Resume | "BCom, Quantitative Finance & Statistics, Economics Minor" |
| Rotman peers | "Finance, stats, and CS - building quant systems" |
| Interviews | "Rotman Commerce with technical focus" |
| Never say | "Management Specialist" |

## Internal Rotman Positioning

When asked about specialist, respond with identity not program:

> "I'm doing Finance, Stats, and CS - building quant systems"

This reframes based on what you do, not what box you're in.

## Year 2 Plan

**Fall:** MAT223, CSC148, STA257 (foundations)

**Winter:** MAT235/237, CSC207, STA261 (build on fall)

**Year 3-4:** Go deeper on ML, advanced stats, or audit grad courses

## CFM Comparison

Had 98.5 grade 12 average and 99 in all math courses. Could have gotten into Waterloo CFM but didn't know about it until February of grade 12 when applications closed.

**CFM reframe:** CFM is a conveyor belt that produces good outcomes on average. This custom path can produce exceptional outcomes if executed. Agilith internship in first year already exceeds what most CFM students have until 2nd or 3rd year co-op.

## Benchmarking

Comparison group: Three best friends at Waterloo (2 SWE, 1 Math/BBA). This maintains technical ambition benchmark rather than Rotman average.
