# Negotiation Strategy: Two Offers in Hand

## The Situation

Two opportunities:
1. Agilith Capital — quant research internship
2. SF Startup — early-stage opportunity

## Strategy: SF Number First

Get the SF startup number before negotiating with Agilith. Don't volunteer the SF opportunity unless asked.

### Why

- SF startup number gives you anchoring data
- Two offers in hand = maximum leverage
- Agilith already signaled flexibility ("willing to invest," "define what you need")
- Competing offers drive up the number

## If Asked About Other Opportunities

> "I'm exploring one other opportunity — an early-stage startup — but nothing's finalized. I'm more interested in the research depth here."

Don't volunteer. If directly asked, be honest but low-key. Frame it as exploratory, not as leverage.

## If Not Asked

Don't mention it. Focus on the work, the research depth, the learning opportunity.

## After Getting SF Number

1. Anchor with both numbers
2. Let Agilith respond first if possible
3. The $1,000 CAD/week offer already exceeds initial $35-40/hour target (converted to weekly)
4. Hardware provision ($4,650 CAD) adds to total comp value

## Signal Reading

Watch for these signals during negotiation:
- "Flexible on comp" = budget not constrained, you have room
- "Define what you need" = they want to accommodate, not lowball
- "Long-term if it works" = they see you as potential hire beyond internship

## Outcome

SF startup handled as leverage without being explicit about it. Agilith came in at $1,000 CAD/week plus full hardware. Total comp value exceeds initial targets.
