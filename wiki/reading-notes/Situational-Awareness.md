# Situational Awareness

Leopold Aschenbrenner's public essay on the decade ahead (June 2024). Foundational source for Leopold trading thesis.

## Core Claims

- **AGI by 2025-27**: GPT-2 → GPT-4 was ~4 years, ~preschooler to smart high schooler. Another ~100,000x effective compute by 2027 = another GPT-2-to-GPT-4-sized jump → AGI
- **OOM counting**: Compute ~0.5 OOMs/year, algorithmic efficiencies ~0.5 OOMs/year, unhobbling gains additional
- **Intelligence explosion**: AGI automates AI research → 100M+ AI researchers running 24/7 → compresses 5+ OOMs of algorithmic progress into 1 year → superintelligence
- **$1T clusters by 2030**: 100GW power (~20% of US electricity), 100M GPUs
- **Government involvement by 2027-28**: USG wakes up, "The Project" begins

## Three Categories of Scaleup

1. **Compute**: GPU clusters growing ~0.5 OOMs/year
2. **Algorithmic efficiencies**: ~0.5 OOMs/year (compute multipliers)
3. **Unhobbling**: Tool use, agents, removing artificial constraints

## The Five Infrastructure Bottlenecks (implicit)

Document emphasizes:
1. **Power**: Grid capacity, voltage transformers, power contracts — "fierce scramble to secure every power contract still available"
2. **Compute**: GPU supply, cluster construction
3. **Data center infrastructure**: Land, permitting, construction
4. **Chip fabrication**: TSMC capacity (implied)
5. **Cooling/networking**: (implied by cluster requirements)

## Key Quotes

> "By the end of the decade, American electricity production will have grown tens of percent; from the shale fields of Pennsylvania to the solar farms of Nevada, hundreds of millions of GPUs will hum."

> "$100B+ cluster, slated for 2028 — a cost comparable to the International Space Station"

> "Securing power, land, permitting, and datacenter construction" is the binding constraint

## Relevance to Leopold

This document is the **intellectual foundation** for the Leopold thesis. The five bottlenecks (power, compute, memory, storage, optical) map directly to the infrastructure buildout described here.
