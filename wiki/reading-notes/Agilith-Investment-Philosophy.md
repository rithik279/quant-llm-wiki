# Investment Philosophy: Three-Layer Framework

Core thesis: combining quantitative, LLM, and fundamental investing into one system.

## Three Investment Layers

**Layer 1: Quantitative Models**
- Timeframe: short to mid-term signals
- Role: Systematic signal generation
- Strength: Fast, consistent, rule-based

**Layer 2: LLM / AI Analysis**
- Timeframe: mid-term interpretation
- Role: Process text, filings, sentiment
- Strength: Qualitative signal extraction

**Layer 3: Fundamental Investing (Patrick / team)**
- Timeframe: long-term conviction (5-8 year horizon)
- Role: Deep value, thesis-driven
- Strength: Edge in structural themes

**Goal:** Combine all three into unified system.

## Additional Alpha Concept: Money Flow Analysis

**Thesis:** Large players accumulate or distribute stock before price reflects it.

**Implication:** If you can detect institutional money flow, you get early signal before the price move.

**Use case:** Confirm or invalidate price-based signals. If price is up but money flow shows distribution → warning signal.

**Connection to Leopold:** Bottleneck winners attract institutional capital early. Money flow could be a leading indicator of bottleneck rotation.

## Strategy Concepts (Broader than Momentum)

1. **Momentum-based strategy** — identify early-stage momentum, combine with fundamentals
2. **Regime-based strategy** — classify market conditions, adjust strategies accordingly
3. **Pairs trading** — identify correlated stocks, exploit divergence
4. **Options overlay** — use LEAPS on high conviction ideas

**Options strategy:** 95/5 split (5% in LEAPs on max-conviction names) → convexity without material drawdown risk.

## Monte Carlo Simulation (Specific Use)

**Purpose:** Forward-looking probability estimation.

**Inputs:** Trade distributions

**Outputs:**
- Simulated equity paths
- Probability of hitting profit thresholds
- Probability of hitting drawdown limits

**Use:** Validate strategy robustness before deployment. Not just for validation — for forward-looking estimation.

**Also:** Monte Carlo + regime forecasting using HMM transition probabilities.

## Overfitting as Feature

**Insight:** If a strategy overfits a specific period, identify what conditions made it work → reuse when conditions repeat.

**Pattern library:** Build database of condition-strategy pairs. Track when regimes repeat → activate appropriate strategies.

**Connection to Regime modeling:** Same idea — market conditions cycle, strategies that work in specific regimes can be reused.

## Barbel Structure (Leopold Connection)

**Side 1:** Infrastructure / picks-and-shovels
**Side 2:** Agent / application / asymmetric upside

**Adaptive allocation** between sides based on where bottleneck is.

## Core Insight: Bottleneck Movement

From AI paper chat:
- Bottlenecks shift over time
- Edge comes from tracking where bottleneck moves next

**Application:**
- When power bottleneck is tight → infrastructure side
- When compute bottleneck eases → application side
- Money flow could signal bottleneck rotation early
