# Superalignment

The unsolved problem: how to control AI systems smarter than us.

## The Core Problem

> "Reliably controlling AI systems much smarter than we are is an unsolved technical problem."

We don't know how to ensure superintelligent AI does what we want. This is not just about following rules — it's about value alignment at superhuman capability levels.

## Why It's Hard

1. **We can't audit superintelligence** — "AI systems vastly smarter than humans, capable of novel, creative, complicated behavior we couldn't even begin to understand"
2. **Capability generalization** — smarter systems find unexpected solutions
3. **The alignment problem compounds** — harder at every capability level
4. **Speed vs caution tradeoff** — race dynamics push labs to move fast

## The Risk

> "Failure could easily be catastrophic."

This is the tail risk for the entire AI enterprise. But note: this is a risk, not a certainty. The document assumes the problem is solvable.

## Timeline Tension

- AI progress is extremely fast (OOMs per year)
- Alignment research takes time
- Labs are racing each other + with China
- "Things could very easily go off the rails during a rapid intelligence explosion"

## Implications for Leopold

Superalignment is a risk factor, but not necessarily a trading signal:
- **Downside scenario**: alignment failure → AI development slows or halts → infrastructure thesis impaired
- **Baseline scenario**: alignment achieved → everything continues
- **Upside scenario**: N/A for trading purposes

**Key question**: Does alignment failure risk change the 5-year infrastructure investment thesis? Probably not — the infrastructure buildout has independent momentum from commercial AI.
