# AI Supply Chain: Extended Stock Universe

## Beyond the 5 Core Holdings

The 5 core holdings (BE, CRWV, LITE, MU, SNDK) cover primary bottlenecks. The AI supply chain extends to additional layers.

## Semiconductor Universe

### Under-the-Radar Stocks

- **CRDO** — Credo Technology (SerDes, optical)
- **SIMO** — Silicon Motion (NAND controllers)
- **SYNA** — Synaptics (human interface, touch)
- **ASYS** — Amtech Systems (semiconductor equipment)

### Undervalued Mid-Caps

- **MU** — Micron (forward P/E ~14)
- **ON** — ON Semiconductor
- **QCOM** — Qualcomm

## AI Supply Chain Layers

| Layer | Bottleneck | Tickers |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Advanced Packaging | CoWoS sold out through 2027 | AMKR, ASX |
| Power/Grid | 5-year interconnect queues | VRT, ETN |
| Liquid Cooling | 1000W+ GPUs require it | VRT, MOD |
| Nuclear/Uranium | Hyperscalers signing PPAs | CCJ, CEG |
| Copper | 500k+ tons/year demand by 2030 | FCX |

### Layer 1: Advanced Packaging

**Bottleneck:** CoWoS (Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate) packaging capacity sold out through 2027. TSMC can't package HBM fast enough.

**Plays:**
- **AMKR** — Amkor Technology (outsourced semiconductor assembly/test)
- **ASX** — ASE Technology (advanced packaging)

### Layer 2: Power/Grid

**Bottleneck:** 5-year queues for grid interconnections. Power delivery infrastructure behind compute buildout.

**Plays:**
- **VRT** — Vertiv (power distribution, cooling)
- **ETN** — Eaton (electrical distribution)

### Layer 3: Liquid Cooling

**Bottleneck:** NVIDIA Blackwell (1000W+ per GPU) requires liquid cooling. Air cooling insufficient.

**Plays:**
- **VRT** — Vertiv (liquid cooling systems)
- **MOD** — Moderna? No — **LIAN** — Liion? No — actual liquid cooling plays TBD

### Layer 4: Nuclear/Uranium

**Bottleneck:** Hyperscalers signing long-term PPAs (Power Purchase Agreements) for nuclear power. Grid power insufficient; need dedicated generation.

**Plays:**
- **CCJ** — Cameco (uranium)
- **CEG** — Constellation Energy (nuclear, already in portfolio as emerging)

### Layer 5: Copper

**Bottleneck:** AI infrastructure requires 500k+ tons/year copper by 2030. Grid buildout, data centers, wiring all demand copper.

**Plays:**
- **FCX** — Freeport-McMoRan (copper miner)

## Investment Thesis Connection

These supply chain plays extend the bottleneck thesis beyond core holdings:
- Core holdings: Direct plays on primary bottlenecks
- Supply chain: Equipment, materials, and infrastructure supporting the buildout

The "bottleneck shift" insight applies here too — as primary bottlenecks get priced in, supply chain constraints become the next alpha source.
