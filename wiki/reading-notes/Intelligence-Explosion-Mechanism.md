# The Intelligence Explosion — Deep Mechanism

AlphaGo analogy and how recursive improvement works.

## AlphaGo Story

After learning from the best human games, AlphaGo started playing against itself. Quickly became superhuman. Played extremely creative and complex moves human would never have come up with.

Same will happen with AI research.

## The Numbers

| Researcher Type | Count | Speed | Quality |
|-----------------|-------|-------|---------|
| Human frontier researchers | ~10k | 1x | 1x |
| AGIs automating research | 100M+ | 10x | 10-100x |

> "Jason says, 'what I do know is that the people who can do this are surely 10-100x AI researchers.'"

## Yolo Runs

Jason's term: getting experiments right on first try based on intuition. Superhuman intuition from training on millions of experiments.

## Bottleneck: Compute for Experiments

More researchers ≠ more throughput if compute-limited. But automated researchers can:
1. Think 1000x more than humans
2. Think faster
3. Build "basic science of predicting large scale results from smaller scale experiments"
4. Get results right after seeing first 1% of training

## The Compression

Normal: ~0.5 OOMs/year algorithmic progress
With AI research: 5+ OOMs in 1 year

This compresses a decade into a year.

## Why Labs Are Ready

- Already have hundreds of millions of GPUs for inference
- Can run 100M+ AGIs
- Each AGI = remote worker doing ML research

## The End State

Vastly superhuman AI systems. "Perhaps even a small civilization of billions of them." Capable of novel, creative, complicated behavior we couldn't even begin to understand.
